Antikythera Survey Project (ASP)
Last Updated 28.11.11
Andrew Bevan

The accompanying dataset contains information about a series of ca.175 petrographic thin sections made from pottery collected by the Antikythera Survey Project in 2005-7 (www.ucl.ac.uk/asp). The data is in comma-delimited format (.csv) and an accompanying .csvt file offers simple field type designators for use with GDAL/OGR (see http://www.gdal.org/ogr/drv_csv.html). It is provided under a Creative Commons CC0 1.0 license (http://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/). For further information on individual data fields, please refer to the attribute field dictionary below:

"TSID" - a unique identifier for the thin section composed (sometimes also referred to with a ASP prefix in ASP publications).

"UID" - a unique identifier for the sherd, composed from either unit-walker-pass-section-sherd (for those sherds collected during stage-one tractwalking) or square-section-sherd (for those sherds collected during stage-two gridded collections)

"Refiring" -  The Munsell colour of the sample after refiring at 1000¡C in oxidising conditions.

"FabricType" -  a description of the fabric group to which the sherd belongs

"Coarseness" - a description of the coarseness of the fabric 

"OptActivity" - an assessment of groundmass optical activity
